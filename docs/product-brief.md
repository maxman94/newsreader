# Product Brief

## Vision

Build a daily digest app that replaces compulsive scrolling with a finite,
beautiful reading ritual.

The app should feel like a quiet, well-edited publication assembled for one
person each day. The tone is deliberate and literary. Think New Yorker,
bookish magazine, or weekend review section, not tabloids, social feeds, or
content farms.

## Core promise

Users open the app once or a few times per day, read a bounded digest, mark
items complete, and leave feeling informed rather than depleted.

## Primary user goals

- Get caught up on major news without entering infinite feeds.
- Mix short-form headlines with one or two longer reading experiences.
- Include a small amount of leisure media alongside news.
- Preserve a stable record of what was shown each day.
- Build a repeatable habit around completion.

## Product principles

- Finite over infinite. Every digest has a clear end.
- Elegant over noisy. Layout, motion, and typography should support reading.
- Deterministic over ephemeral. Once generated, a digest should be stable.
- Configurable over overwhelming. Users can tune the digest without turning the
  product into a dashboard.
- Editorial over algorithmic. Selection may be automated, but it should feel
  curated, not optimized for engagement.

## Target audience

Primary audience:

- Readers who want to be informed but dislike the emotional cost of social or
  feed-driven news consumption.
- Users who value routine, aesthetics, and reading quality.
- Users who consume both current events and a small daily serving of culture.

Secondary audience:

- Users who want one place for short news, essays, music discovery, comics, and
  backlog-based long-form reading.

## Product pillars

### 1. Daily digest

The home experience is a dated digest for today. It is intentionally bounded.
There should be a visible sense of progress through the digest and a visible
end state.

### 2. Plugin-based content

The digest is assembled from plugins chosen and configured by the user. Each
plugin owns a content slot or page and can provide its own configuration UI.

### 3. Personalization without clutter

Users can choose theme, contrast, type settings, and plugin mix. Personalization
must remain simple enough that the app still feels editorial.

### 4. Archive and habits

Users can revisit past digest snapshots and see completion history by day and
plugin.

## Initial release goals

- Users can sign in with Google.
- Users can configure a daily digest composed of core plugins.
- The app generates and stores a dated digest snapshot.
- Users can read and complete each plugin section.
- Users can revisit prior digest dates.
- Users can review habit completion in a matrix view.

## Success indicators

- High digest completion rate.
- Repeat usage on consecutive days.
- Low average number of plugin slots skipped.
- Healthy archive revisit behavior.
- Low settings churn after initial configuration.
